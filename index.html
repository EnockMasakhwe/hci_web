Real Madrid Club de Fútbol, founded in 1902, has grown into one of the world's most successful and iconic football clubs.
Initially dominant in Spain, Real Madrid rose to international prominence in the 1950s, winning the inaugural European Cup and five consecutive titles.
Led by legends like Alfredo Di Stéfano, they established themselves as a powerhouse in European football.

In subsequent decades, Real Madrid experienced highs and lows but maintained a formidable presence both domestically and internationally.
The early 2000s saw the "Galácticos" era under Florentino Pérez, with high-profile signings and renewed success.
The club's Santiago Bernabéu Stadium has been their iconic home since 1947.

Real Madrid's trophy cabinet includes 34 La Liga titles, 19 Copa del Rey titles, and a record 13 UEFA Champions League/European Cup victories as of 2024.
Throughout its history, the club has embodied a commitment to excellence, consistently attracting top talent and achieving remarkable success on the global stage.
